What is the perfect tense in German?

Sep 9, 2024 The German perfect tense consists of an auxiliary verb and a past participle. Auxiliary verbs can be sein or haben. The choice depends on the main verb. The auxiliary verb must be conjugated in the present tense according to the subject. The Perfekt (present perfect) is one of three German past tenses (the others being the Präteritum, in English simple past, and the Plusquamperfekt, past perfect). If you want to dive into the German past tenses, we have some good news for you: The German perfect tense is the most commonly used and easiest of all and a good point to start.
