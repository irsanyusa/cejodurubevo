Best Luggage Sets for Every Kind of Traveler

Many of us may fantasize about hopping on a plane or train with nothing but a small carry-on bag, unburdened and ready for adventure. Or perhaps we just want to hit the open road with nothing but a change of clothes and a good soundtrack. But while this style of packing may seem light and free, the reality of travel usually necessitates bringing along a few more items… possibly even two suitcases’ worth, between the carry-on and the checked bag. If you travel for extended periods, a larger suitcase can keep you prepared and supplied with everything you need, while a carry-on is great for shorter trips or additional packing room. So why not save money and time by buying a luggage set?

Luggage sets are a cost-effective, versatile way to pack for your next trip. Buying a set ensures that all your bags meet your needs, while looking cohesive. You won’t need to bring the full set on every trip, depending on how long you go for and who you’re traveling with, but owning a luggage set gives you options when it comes to deciding what to bring. Bundling suitcases together is always more affordable than buying pieces individually, and you can put those savings towards your next trip.

Of course, different kinds of travelers will look for different qualities in their luggage. I’ve put together this list of the best luggage sets to address a variety of needs and priorities.

Tips for buying a luggage set

Consider how many pieces of luggage are included for the price and whether there are additional products you can add on as needed. While there are some great value sets of three to four pieces, it may not be worth buying—and storing—additional bags if you won’t get regular use out of them. Also be sure to check the dimensions of the suitcases, as there will be fluctuations in size between brands; some carry-ons measure 19 inches while others come in at 21 inches, so make sure you’re getting the size you need.

Consider the materials. Hardside luggage sets will usually be made of either ABS or 100% polycarbonate; ABS is cheaper but a little less durable, which may influence your choice. Softside luggage is usually surface-treated for durability and water resistance, but might require more intense cleaning than hardside sets, which can just be wiped down. However, in the long run, softside fabrics usually hold up better thanks to their greater flexibility. Finally, think about whether you want something that is eye-catching and easy to spot at baggage claim, or whether you want something more restrained and stylish. There’s something for everyone on this list.

However, if you are planning to share this set with a partner or with your family, you might need additional features or capacity. Families may want to look for luggage sets with three or four pieces to better accommodate each family member, while couples may want a customizable set that allows for two carry-ons and one checked bag. Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What usually comes in a luggage set?

Most luggage sets consist of either two or three pieces of luggage: a carry-on bag and then either a medium or large checked bag (or both). While there are sets that focus on carry-on only, such as an upright wheelie bag and a separate personal item, I prioritized luggage sets that featured greater storage space. The luggage sets are usually standard in color, although a couple of brands let you mix and match colors within a set. It’s often possible to purchase additional products in the same collection, in addition to the luggage set, such as a second carry-on bag, a duffel bag or a larger sized checked bag.

What are the benefits of luggage sets?

Buying luggage in a set (as opposed to individually) has a couple of key benefits. For one, brands usually offer a discounted price when you buy multiple items together; larger sets or more expensive sets can have savings worth hundreds of dollars. Another benefit is the luggage matches and share the same aesthetic if they’re purchased as a set. While you may be able to buy additional matching pieces at a later date, this could prove challenging if there is a lack of inventory or the brand changes its colorways. Lastly, buying a luggage set is a sure way to get several different pieces of luggage in complementary sizes, so you always have something to fit your packing needs.

How many pieces should a luggage set have?

This depends on your needs. If you’re a solo traveler or buying for one person, you will probably only need a two-piece luggage set consisting of a carry-on bag and a checked bag. Couples may want the option of a third matching piece, so both individuals can have a carry-on and they can share the checked bag. Families may be okay with three pieces or they may want to look for luggage sets that offer additional items. You can always customize your set and add on another piece of luggage, but the best discounts will come from official sets so look for brands that offer the number of suitcases that best suit your travel needs.

Can you create your own set?

Most brands will offer several different luggage combinations, including different size options and different color options. This enables you to find the mix of suitcases that best serves you, although it may not be possible to get exactly the combination you want if you’re looking for an unusual mix of items. Some brands let you build your own set by adding individual products to your basket, with the set discount being applied at checkout.
